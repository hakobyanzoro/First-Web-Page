Hollywood movies for thos, who are tired of banal scenes

In every second movie from a large screen, the plot, if it doesn’t see a hole in a sieve, becomes damn predictable from the very first minutes.
The AdMe.ru editors got hungry for unusual good films and prepared a selection with films that the language doesn’t turn out to be called banal.
A very kind and bright girl named Skunk (Stinker) has diabetes, she lives with her father in a rather dysfunctional area of London.
Her neighbors are mired in meaningless hatred to each other, and Skank madly wants to reconcile these broken lives. The film is not at all sugary,
quite realistic with an unfamiliar plot and a strong ending.

Every day, a woman named Ila sends her husband a meal in a lunchbox, which the peddler delivers to work. But one day, dinner mistakenly gets to
another man, with whom Elah starts a correspondence. The picture is laconic, but at the same time very expressive, seemingly sharply social, but
unobtrusively dramatic. A film that every poor man will always strive for happiness, no matter how hard his life in the present.

In the near future, single people are arrested and sent to a hotel, where they are obliged to find a couple for 45 days. Otherwise, they will be
turned into animals and released into the forest. In this case, whom they turn into, non-private can choose themselves. To everyone's surprise,
the protagonist decides to become ... a lobster. This initially hopeless dystopia imperceptibly leads the viewer to the conclusion that
sincere love demolishes all barriers set up by society.
